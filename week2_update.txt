Cluster Evaluation:
A2A Scores - Very high across all pairs (WCA/Limbo: 92.6%, WCA/ACDC: 92.1%).
CVG Scores - Extremely low across all pairs (0.25).
Conclusion: 
	The high A2A scores are a mathematical illusion caused by all three algorithms agreeing on one massive core component. 
	However, the CVG reveals complete disagreement on granular components. 
	Limbo and ACDC distribute the remaining classes logically, while WCA collapses due to the snowball effect.

LLM Hyperparameter & Prompt Tuning:
	Token Limit - 512 tokens was insufficient and caused cut-offs; increased to 1024.
	Logic/Math Hallucinations - Standard zero-shot prompting caused the LLM to hallucinate loop iterations (guessing 3 digits instead of 4).
	Greedy Decoding -  Setting do_sample=False (Greedy Decoding) fixed the math but resulted in a robotic tone unsuitable for architectural summaries.
Conclusion: 
	We maintained a slight creativity threshold (temp=0.3, top_p=0.8) but applied Chain of Thought prompting by explicitly instructing the LLM to trace variables in each loop iteration. 
	This forced the model to show its work, resulting in more accurate logic with a natural tone.
